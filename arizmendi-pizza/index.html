A cooperative, not a chain

Every baker here is an owner.

Arizmendi is a worker-owned cooperative named for José María Arizmendiarrieta, the priest who helped inspire the Mondragón Cooperatives in the Basque Country in the 1950s. Decisions, profits and dough are shared. We are two blocks from Golden Gate Park, and we've been at this since the year 2000.

Morning pastries come out first. Pizza goes in at eleven. Breads rotate by day of the week — Friday is challah and fig-fennel. The menu above is today's truth; tomorrow will be a slightly different truth.

— the crew on 9th Ave.
